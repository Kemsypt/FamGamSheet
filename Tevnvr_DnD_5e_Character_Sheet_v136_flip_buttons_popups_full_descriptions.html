How to use Familiars

A familiar follows the normal action rules for creatures in D&D 5e, with one major restriction: it cannot take the Attack action. Everything else depends on its stat block and the situation.

Here are the key actions a familiar can perform:

Standard Actions

Dash

Move extra distance for the turn.

Disengage

Move without provoking opportunity attacks.

Dodge

Impose disadvantage on attacks against it and gain advantage on Dex saves until next turn.

Help

Grant advantage to an ally:

  • In combat: give advantage on an attack against a creature within 5 ft of the familiar
  • Outside combat: assist with tasks (DM discretion)

Hide

Attempt to become unseen (Stealth check required).

Ready

Prepare an action to trigger later, such as “Help when the fighter attacks”.

Search

Actively look for hidden creatures or objects using Perception or Investigation.

Use an Object

Interact with items such as opening doors, carrying small objects, or pressing switches, depending on the DM.

Deliver a Touch Spell (Reaction)

When you cast a touch spell, your familiar can deliver it if within 100 ft, using its reaction.

Scout / Perceive

Often involves Search or passive perception while you observe through its senses.

What It Cannot Do

  • Attack — explicitly forbidden by the spell.
  • Take actions its form physically can’t perform, such as opening doors without limbs unless the DM allows it.

Practical Notes

  • Help is the most important combat action. Familiars are often used to grant advantage every round.
  • Disengage + movement = survivability positioning. Especially relevant for flying familiars like owls.
  • Hide + scouting = core utility. Most familiars shine outside combat.

Familiar Type

When you summon a familiar, its spirit can be Celestial, Fey, or Fiend. This changes its creature type without changing the familiar form’s basic stat block.

Celestial

A radiant or divine spirit. Useful for holy, guardian, or otherworldly flavour.

Fey

A whimsical, arcane, or nature-touched spirit. This fits Tevnyr’s fey theme especially well.

Fiend

A darker spirit with infernal or abyssal flavour. The familiar still follows the same Find Familiar restrictions.

Sneak Attack

Once per turn, you can deal an extra 4d6 damage to one creature you hit with an attack if you have advantage on the attack roll. The attack must use a finesse or a ranged weapon.

You don't need advantage on the attack roll if another enemy of the target is within 5 feet of it, that enemy isn't incapacitated, and you don't have disadvantage on the attack roll.

Two-Weapon Fighting

This can’t be used with Green-Flame Blade.

When you take the Attack action using a light melee weapon in one hand (such as a dagger, shortsword, scimitar, or handaxe), you can use a bonus action to make an extra attack with a different light melee weapon in your other hand.

You don’t add your ability modifier to the damage of this bonus attack, unless the modifier is negative.

If a weapon has the thrown property (like a dagger or handaxe), you can throw it instead of making a melee attack with it.
